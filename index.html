About

Significance and Description

Significance

This internal mini-symposium provides a focused, interdisciplinary platform for members of the Chen Jun-An Laboratory and invited collaborators working across epigenetics, RNA biology, and neurodevelopment. It is designed to support trainees and early-career scientists in developing scientific perspectives, strengthening technical expertise, and engaging with current research advances.

Through an interactive environment, the event offers opportunities to learn from ongoing work, exchange ideas, and build professional connections, thereby fostering future academic development and enhancing research capacity and innovation in the field.

Description

Hosted by the Chen Jun-An Laboratory at the Institute of Molecular Biology, Academia Sinica, this three-day, training-oriented mini-symposium is designed to provide both conceptual insights and practical knowledge relevant to ongoing research in epigenetic regulation and neural development.

The program will feature:

  • Research talks from the Academia Sinica Chen Jun-An Laboratory and National Cheng Kung University Chen Bai-Sheng Laboratory
  • Methodological sessions covering molecular, cellular, and biochemical approaches
  • Interactive discussions, lab activities, and focused exchange time to facilitate knowledge sharing and potential collaboration

Topics will include, but are not limited to:

  • Long non-coding RNA-mediated gene regulation
  • Chromatin dynamics in neural differentiation
  • Experimental platforms for studying neurodegenerative disease models

Presentation Brief

Presentation Guidelines

01

Start from the ideal endpoint

Each member is asked to imagine the project under ideal conditions: unlimited budget, sufficient resources, smooth collaboration, mature technology, and no practical constraints. What experiment would you most want to complete, what key result would define the project, and how far could the question be pushed?

02

Reverse-plan back to reality

After defining the ideal endpoint, work backward to identify a feasible but ambitious path. Consider which steps are essential, which can be removed, what resources should be integrated early, and which collaborations or experimental designs can make the path faster and sharper.

03

Clarify the next strategic move

This symposium is not a standard progress report. Each presentation should help clarify three points: what you truly want to accomplish, what currently limits the project most, and what should change next to raise both the speed and ambition of the work.

Jun-An Chen

Jun-An Chen is a tenured research fellow at the Institute of Molecular Biology, Academia Sinica. His laboratory investigates non-coding RNA function during neural development, degeneration, and aging, integrating developmental biology, neuroscience, and systems-level approaches.

Host

Po-Sheng Chen

Po-Sheng Chen is a professor at National Cheng Kung University. His laboratory investigates how hypoxia-inducible factor HIF-1α reshapes non-coding RNA landscapes to reprogram gene expression and translation in cancer, integrating noncanonical RNA biology, molecular mechanisms of ncRNA regulation, and translational biomarker development.
